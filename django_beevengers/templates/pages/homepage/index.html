Alaska Beekeeping

All about cold weather beekeeping in the 49th state

About

This Website and accompanying Facebook page is for Alaska beekeepers (Alaska Beeks) and others seeking information about keeping honey bees, and maintaining honey bee health, in the 49th state. The honey-producing season in Alaska is brief, compared to seasons in other states, and after a mild spring and a wet and sunny summer, the calendar is rounded out by sometimes bone-chilling temperatures during our long winter months. This page is primarily intended as an encouragement for those involved and considering the art, informed by science, of beekeeping. This can become a welcoming question-and-answer forum for all of us. Most of all we want to encourage and help Alaska beeks be successful. Please join us in dialogs, questions and answers, about beekeeping in Alaska. We'd like to see this Website and Facebook page grow by means of a grassroots growth efforts!

Bio

Barbara Bachmeier is a retired US Army officer, who is originally from Eugene, Oregon. She is a graduate of the University of Oregon (Go Ducks!) and the University of Virginia (Wahoowa!) and has taken numerous classes in beekeeping over the years; her next goal is to complete the Master Beekeeper program at the University of Montana. After retirement and with time on her hands, she has made her home and continued her adventures in Southcentral Alaska. Barbara has been a honey bee enthusiast for ten years, planting pollinator friendly gardens and sitting with bees as part of a Zen practice. For the last six years, she has maintained a year round apiary in her east Anchorage backyard. She also has a small business, Anchorage Urban Farm and Apiary, where she sells raw Alaska honey; 100% Alaska beeswax; and pure local pollen within Alaska and by USPS shipments Outside. Starting in February each year, Barbara offers introductory beekeeping classes and mentoring assistance. Besides keeping honey bees, she keeps free-range chickens and sells eggs. Feel free to contact her with your questions or to order honey bee products.
